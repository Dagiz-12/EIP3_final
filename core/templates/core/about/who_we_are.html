    Brief History

    Ethiopian Institute of Peace for Ethiopia and the Horn of Africa

    Ethiopian Institute of Peace (EIP) for Ethiopia and the Horn of Africa, established in 1991, is a non-governmental, nonpartisan and nonprofit making organization that grew out of the Ad hoc Peace Committee (AHPC).

    Historically, EIP is known to be the first NGO that was founded by native Ethiopian leadership that works in peace building and conflict resolution. The AHPC was established in December 1989-1990 to promote peace, mediation and reconciliation through dialogue and negotiation among the Ethiopian civil war conflicting parties of 1974-90.

    Our Founding Vision

    The process began at a critical period of Ethiopian history when the nation sought indigenous mechanisms for consolidating various peace initiatives and resolving conflicts through dialogue, power sharing, co-existence and reconciliation.

    The Journey of Peace Building

    Sensing this critical need and prompted by those close to the conflicting parties, the Ad-hoc Committee for Peace (AHPC) was formally founded in 1990 by a neutral group of elders and mediators who came from diverse Ethio-Eritrean ethnic and religious backgrounds.

    Mediation & Dialogue

    Promoting peace through negotiation and reconciliation among conflicting parties.

    Community Leadership

    Founded by native Ethiopian leadership focused on sustainable peace building.

    International Collaboration

    To fulfill its mission, the Committee solicited the technical assistance and financial support from the Life and Peace Institute (LPI), a Sweden-based humanitarian organization. To ensure international acceptability and enhance its credibility, the Committee also sought technical assistance from LPI's partner organizations.

    Key Milestones:

    • First meeting as a group in February 1991 at Princeton, New Jersey
    • Direct contact established with all political organizations and conflicting parties
    • All-party meeting scheduled in Caux, Switzerland in early June 1991

    Transition and Evolution

    The Ad Hoc Committee was supposed to disband when peace had been achieved in Ethiopia. However, it was felt among the group of Ethiopians and the international partners that the work involved was not of a temporary nature.

    "Search for peace involves continued engagement; and it was felt that peace and development go hand in hand."

    Thus, AHCPD was established in Ethiopia in January 1992. Subsequently, the office changed its name to Peace and Development Committee in 1994 and to Ethiopian Institute of Peace in 2011.

    Our Continuing Mission

    Today, EIP continues to work towards sustainable peace and development through community engagement, capacity building, and partnership development across Ethiopia and the Horn of Africa.
